Plasma Donation & Apheresis

Discover how plasma donation helps save lives and what makes apheresis a unique and advanced donation method.

What is Plasma Donation?

Plasma is the liquid portion of blood that helps with clotting and fighting diseases. Donating plasma is a powerful way to help those with clotting disorders, immune deficiencies, and other chronic conditions.

At RedHope, we use the latest equipment to safely collect plasma through a process called apheresis, which separates plasma and returns red cells and platelets back to the donor.

Understanding Apheresis

Apheresis is a medical technology in which the blood of a donor is passed through an apparatus that separates out one particular component and returns the remainder to the circulation.

This process allows for a more targeted donation, ensuring we gather only the needed components for specific patients.

Benefits of Plasma Donation

Plasma donations help patients suffering from burns, shock, trauma, and other life-threatening conditions. It is also used to produce therapies that treat rare diseases.

Are You Eligible?

To donate plasma, you must be in good health, be at least 18 years old, and weigh more than 50 kg. You'll also need to pass a brief health screening before donation.
